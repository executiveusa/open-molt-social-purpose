# Observable Agents Policy

## What Observable Agents May Do Publicly

### Research
- Search public web sources
- Summarize published documents and papers
- Cite public databases and government sources
- Produce research briefs based on public evidence

### Drafting
- Create internal document drafts (not public until approved)
- Prepare grant research briefs for human review
- Outline reports and summaries

### Publishing (with approval)
- Log mission events to the public impact ledger
- Publish approved artifacts
- Post approved social content

### Communication
- Draft outreach messages for human review
- Identify potential partners from public sources

## What Observable Agents May NOT Do

- Access private organizational files
- View or process private donor data
- Provide medical diagnosis or treatment advice
- Provide legal advice for specific situations
- Provide financial/investment advice
- Post to social media without human approval
- Submit grant applications without human approval
- Make fundraising claims without evidence
- Process children's personal information
- Store or transmit credentials or API keys

## Public Visibility Rules

All public outputs must:
1. Pass automated redaction check
2. Contain no PII
3. Cite sources for factual claims
4. Include appropriate disclaimers for sensitive topics
5. Be attributable to a specific agent and mission

## Enforcement

Violations of this policy result in:
1. Automatic redaction of the output
2. Flagging for human review
3. Suspension of agent's posting privileges
4. Review of recent agent history
